Overview

This Balita newspaper, dated from April 30, 1985, shows the headline "Child Prostitutes: now 20, 000 in RP!!" This shows the issue of at least 20, 000 kidnappings and child-trafficking going rampant under the former Pres. Marcos' Martial Law. Despite the former president promising that his Martial Law will bring peaceful times, the issue of thousands of children being kidnapped and trafficked was ongoing during the time. This was one of the many social issues during the former Pres. Marcos time during his dictatorship. And it shows the inefficiency and lack of justice in his authority and government.

Overview

This Malaya newspaper, dated from Sept 1, 1983, shows the headline "Millions pay last respects to Ninoy". This refers to the event where millions of Filipinos showed respect to Ninoy Aquino, who was at the time recently killed by an assassination attempt at the Manila International Airport (now known as Ninoy Aquino International Airport). This was marked as one of the biggest historical events in the Philippines. And it would later spark the events leading up to the 1986 People's Power Revolution.

This article from the Official Gazette.gov.ph website shows the article titled as "The Fall of the Dictatorship". This refers to the former Pres. Marcos' Dictatorship in the Philippines. He was first-elected in 1965, and then reelected in 1969. He then officially declared Martial Law on the Philippines, which lasted from 1972 to 1986. The article focuses on the social factors leading up to the downfall of former Pres. Marcos and his reign.

Overview

This Balita newspaper, dated from March 17, 1987, shows the headline "Opposition team can't disguise Marcos' past". This refers to when the former Pres. Marcos attempted to run for presidency in the Philippines for a third time during the "Snap" election. The Opposition teams refers to the political party that was against Corazon Aquino's team and supported the former Pres. Marcos. This was a major social issues back then because it caused the nation to be divided politically: those in favor for the former Pres. Marcos, and those against him and his political party.

This 1972 broadcast video shows the former Pres. Ferdinand Marcos discussing the implementation of Martial Law in the Philippines. He claims that it is necessary to stop terrorism and crime in the country at the time. He also claims favorable public reaction and outlines his vision for a "new kind of society" aimed at reforming the country. He makes several points and claims that the new Martial Law will be beneficial towards the people and the country. This was a controversial social issue in the Philippines at the time because the people were divided on his speech. Some people were persuaded and believed that the Martial Law would be beneficial, while others pointed out the harms and long-term issues regarding the Martial Law.
