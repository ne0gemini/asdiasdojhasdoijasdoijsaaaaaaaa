An Economic History of the Philippines by Onofre D. Corpuz explains how the Philippine economy developed from the pre-colonial period through Spanish and American rule. The book discusses early Filipino trade and agriculture and shows how colonial policies and global trade shaped the country’s economic system and social development over time.

Overview The video Essential Truths of Martial Law 1972–1986: The Philippine Economy, produced by the Freedom Memorial Museum, provides a critical overview of the economic conditions in the Philippines during Ferdinand Marcos’s Martial Law regime. It examines the structural weaknesses of the economy, including crony capitalism, corruption, and mismanagement of public funds, and highlights the severe crisis of the early 1980s marked by debt, inflation, and unemployment. The presentation underscores how fiscal and monetary policies failed to stabilize the economy and instead deepened inequality and poverty. By situating these economic realities within the broader context of authoritarian rule, the video emphasizes the lasting consequences of Martial Law on the nation’s economic and social fabric, serving as both a historical record and an educational resource for understanding the intersection of governance and economic decline.
